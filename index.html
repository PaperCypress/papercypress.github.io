Sense of Nowhere

VRAnimation

Sense of Nowhere is a VR animated film directed by Hsin Hsuan Yeh. Blurring between different sensations, materials and ideas, its almost meditative journey takes viewers through subconscious landscapes and dioramas using hand gestures inspired by local religion.

Worked as an animation consultant before leading development and visual effects for 3 of the 4 chapters.

The Time Machine

VRAnimation

The Time Machine is an animated VR film about passion and the perception of time. The film features parallel storytelling and aims to be rewatchable and directs the viewers gaze through visual and audio cues.

Sole developer & engineer in a creative team comprised of artists and an animator.

The Room of Myth

VRAnimation

A collaborative exhibition on the Taiwanese superstitions and culture surounding them. My work involves two of the installations and design of the projection areas of the space.

A glitching computer monitor that fixes itself through proximity to a bag of "Guai-Guai", a Taiwanese snack. Modified PS3 Eye, IR leds

A rear-projected table recreates the aesthetics of a Taiwanese Ouiji Board, serving as a interactive guide to the exhibit.

A Shimmer, placed in a corner角落微光

InstallationProjectionAudiovisual

Head coupled perspective anamorphosis using a Kinect and projector

Natural light, eyelids, sparks, nerves, noise, sand. Tactile elements combine to recreate a mental diorama of an invisible illness.
Abstract concepts and feelings may never be represented in our world without the use of multiple sensory modalities.
A Shimmer, placed in a corner uses slices of light and sound to represent the ethereality of our feelings.

、自然的光線、眼皮的顏色、電、神經、雜訊、沙，以各種充滿觸感的元素詮釋無形的病痛中的腦內情境，成為一個物體以擴充實境呈現。
無法捉摸的抽象概念是無法完整且完美的呈現在現實世界中的，需要由多感道結合詮釋。
「角落微光」以光影的切片和聲音呈現感受的飄渺性。
